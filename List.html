Monarch Butterfly

Found in North and South America, this species is famous for its long migrations, where they travel up to 3,000 miles to reach their wintering grounds in Mexico.

Schaus swallowtail

This subspecies of the Schaus swallowtail butterfly is found only in southern Florida, United States. It has black wings with yellow markings and is an endangered species due to habitat loss

Large blue

Found in parts of Europe and Asia. It has blue wings with black spots and is known for its unique life cycle, which involves being adopted by an ant colony as a caterpillar.

Uncompahgre fritillary

Found in North America, specifically in the Rocky Mountains of Colorado and Wyoming. It has orange-brown wings with black spots and can be found in alpine meadows.

Marsh fritillary

Found in Europe and parts of Asia. It is known for its orange and black wings with intricate patterns and can be found in wetland habitats.

Australian azures

Found in Australia, this species has blue-purple wings with black markings and is known for its iridescent coloring.

Glanville fritillary

Found in parts of Europe, this species has orange-brown wings with black markings and is known for its striking geometric patterns.

Large white

Found in Europe, Asia, and parts of Africa, this species has white wings with black markings and is a common garden visitor.

Mazarine Blue

Found in parts of Europe and Asia, this species has blue wings with black spots and a white border.

Madeiran large white

Found only on the island of Madeira, this species is a subspecies of the large white butterfly, with white wings and black markings.

Danube clouded yellow

Found in parts of Europe, this species has yellow wings with black borders and black spots.

Apollo

Found in Europe and Asia, this large and striking butterfly has white wings with black spots and distinctive red markings on its hindwings.
